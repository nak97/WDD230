Hicks Law

Google

Google.com

Hicks law states that the time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices. The above screen shot of the google home page does a great job at showing how to simplify a search page. You can see by how little content there to distract the user from what the page is actually for. This is to make it easier for users to complete their one and only task for using that web page, which is to search for something without a getting distracted.

White Space and Clean Design

Brigham Young University of Idaho

https://www.byui.edu/

The white space and clean design principle simply has to do with how you use your white space within a webpage to make it easy to view the content that you place on your web page. The universities homepage does an excellent job of using white space in a way that makes the website feel clean and not so cluttered. you can see from the screenshot that there is a fair amount of white space but it is used to help space out the content.

PARC: proximity

Sam's Club

https://www.samsclub.com/

proximity is a design principle used to convey ideas by grouping similar items or thoughts close to each other. This helps to convey different thoughts should your content have multiple different ideas. In the example above Sam's Club conveys proximity through grouping coupons together so as to get the attention of the viewer that there are various coupons available in the different catagories and that the viewer should take a look at what there is.
